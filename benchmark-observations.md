# Benchmark Observations

Internal working notes for strengthening the Opun Ecommerce Audit Scanner through repeated comparison of strong and weak ecommerce storefront examples. These notes should stay evidence-led, conservative, and operationally useful.

## Strong Mobile Experiences

- Clear primary action appears early on mobile.
- First-screen mobile content is scannable without excessive competing links or copy.
- Navigation, product discovery, and offer clarity work together before scroll.
- Evidence to record: mobile CTA visibility, first-screen link count, visible text density, mobile crowding risk.

## Weak Mobile Experiences

- Primary mobile action is absent, visually weak, or competing with dense navigation.
- Shoppers may need to interpret too much content before the next step is clear.
- Evidence to record: missing mobile CTA, high first-screen link count, elevated text density, crowding risk.

## Strong CTA Visibility

- CTA labels are specific and visible near the point of decision.
- Mobile and desktop journeys both make the next step easy to identify.
- Evidence to record: CTA count, sampled CTA labels, above-fold mobile CTA detection.

## Weak CTA Visibility

- CTAs are absent, generic, below key decision areas, or visually secondary to navigation.
- Evidence to record: missing above-fold CTA, low CTA count, unclear CTA labels.

## Strong Product Discovery

- Product/category navigation, collection or product links, and search are visible.
- Catalog exploration does not depend only on generic navigation labels.
- Evidence to record: product navigation visibility, collection/product link visibility, search visibility.

## Weak Product Discovery

- Shoppers may need extra steps to understand product categories, collections, or search options.
- Evidence to record: missing product/category navigation, missing collection/product links, missing visible search.

## Strong Trust Visibility

- Multiple reassurance groups are visible, such as reviews, shipping/returns, support, policies, guarantees, or payment trust.
- Trust signals appear close enough to product decisions to support purchase confidence.
- Evidence to record: visible trust-signal count and missing trust groups.

## Weak Trust Visibility

- Reassurance cues are sparse, hidden, or disconnected from the buying decision.
- Evidence to record: low trust-signal count, missing shipping/returns, missing support/contact, missing payment or policy cues.

## Strong Checkout Continuity

- Cart and checkout cues are visible enough to suggest a continuous browse-to-buy path.
- Evidence to record: cart visibility, checkout visibility, cart/checkout label clarity.

## Weak Checkout Continuity

- Cart or checkout entry points are absent, hard to see, or disconnected from product decisions.
- Evidence to record: missing cart visibility, missing checkout visibility, unclear cart-to-checkout path.

## Strong Tracking Visibility

- Multiple analytics, tag manager, pixel, or lifecycle marketing signals are visible.
- Tracking visibility supports follow-up validation of conversion improvements.
- Evidence to record: detected supported marketing tools and lead-capture visibility.

## Weak Tracking Visibility

- Marketing attribution or event visibility appears thin from public-page evidence.
- Evidence to record: no detected supported marketing tools, one-tool tracking stack, unclear lead/event capture.

## Strong Operational Clarity

- Support/contact, shipping, returns, and order communication are visible enough to reduce customer uncertainty.
- Evidence to record: support/contact visibility, order/returns language, shipping/returns language.

## Weak Operational Clarity

- Customers may need to search for help, delivery expectations, returns information, or post-purchase communication.
- Evidence to record: missing support/contact, missing order/returns language, missing shipping/returns messaging.

## Recurring Positive Patterns

- Visible mobile CTA plus controlled first-screen density.
- Product navigation, search, and collection links appearing together.
- Trust, support, and fulfillment language appearing near buying decisions.
- Multiple marketing signals visible before deeper event validation.
- Cart and checkout cues visible in the public storefront journey.

## Recurring Negative Patterns

- Product discovery issues paired with weak search visibility.
- Mobile CTA weakness paired with limited trust visibility.
- Cart or checkout uncertainty paired with weak operational communication.
- Thin tracking visibility paired with conversion recommendations that will be harder to measure.
- Strong platform confidence without enough customer-journey clarity to prioritize fixes.

## Benchmark Notes

- Benchmark tags are directional internal signals, not industry rankings.
- Strong and weak labels should always point back to observed evidence.
- Do not infer percentile position, market leadership, or revenue impact without a real dataset.
- Use benchmark observations to refine recommendation wording, validation review, and future scoring discipline.
